About ParaOps

ParaOps is a tactical airdrop planning tool built for pilots, loadmasters, jumpmasters, and drop zone support teams. It calculates computed air release points (CARPs) for both cargo and personnel operations, overlaid on an interactive map.

Features

  • Cargo Drops — SLP, CDS, PLF, and LE with full ballistic calculations including forward throw, wind drift, rate of fall, and safe DZ time.
  • Personnel Drops — MFF (HALO/HAHO/Combo) and HAPO with freefall drift, canopy drift, HARZ ring computation, and straddle time analysis.
  • Drop Zone Management — Define rectangular or circular DZs with PI coordinates, dimensions, run-in direction, and auto-fetched elevation and magnetic variation.
  • Wind Vectoriser — Enter wind layers at altitude to compute HIBAL and LOBAL ballistic winds in true or magnetic.
  • Weather — GFS forecast data from NOAA NOMADS for wind, temperature, and pressure at your DZ location.
  • Release Point — Automated CARP with radial, distance, run-in heading, ground speed, and escape planning. Jumpmaster RP override supported.
  • Reports & Export — PDF CARP reports with satellite imagery, terrain profiles, and wind data. KML export for OzRunways, ForeFlight, or Google Earth. Shareable .paradrop files.
  • Logbook — Log missions with multi-leg flight times, aircraft callsign, drop events, and GPS trail recording.
  • Terrain Analysis — Elevation data along the run-in track with terrain profile visualisation.
  • Map Tools — Coordinate grid overlay, terrain shading, run-in visualisation with turning point and safe distance markers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What devices does ParaOps support?

ParaOps is built for iPad running iOS 17 or later.

Does the app require an internet connection?

An internet connection is needed to fetch weather data, terrain elevation, and magnetic variation. All other features work offline once data has been retrieved.

Where is my data stored?

All data is stored locally on your device. ParaOps does not transmit personal data to external servers.

How do I share a drop configuration?

Use the export feature to generate a .paradrop file, which can be shared with other ParaOps users via AirDrop, email, or any file sharing method.
